Data first, always

Most crypto content is a guy with an opinion and a rented Lamborghini. My whole approach is the opposite. Men lie, women lie, numbers don't lie, so I let the data tell the truth.

I did the work to understand the data itself. I spent years on data science courses through DataCamp, not to collect a certificate, but to actually understand how data works beyond the price on a chart. Things like statistics, backtesting, and simulation. Then I put it to work on the market.

So I test everything. Popular macro and cycle theories, the indicators people swear by, entry and exit rules, all of it gets run through thousands of backtests and millions of simulated scenarios before I risk a single dollar on it. Most of what sounds smart online does not survive that test. The few things that do are what I actually trade and teach.

The clearest example is public. A full research study on Bitcoin's four-year cycle, with predictions written down in advance instead of claimed after the fact. Read it at bitcoin-daily.com/research.
